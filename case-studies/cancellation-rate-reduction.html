Customer journey optimisation

What is this project about? 

Summary: We faced a huge level of cancellation rate for the onboarding users. We analyzed the whole customer journey, to identify key friction points, and improve the cancellation rate.

2Ô∏è‚É£Problem

New customers too quiclky cancels the app. We wanted to improve the cancellation rate, but we did not know what problem we should fix in order to do that! We had an extremely tight deadline to show some results in the KPIs. Thus, instead of a lenghty research, I decided to apply some UX analysis (with the Hook model) to identify key friction points in the journey, and harness some low hanging fruits to bear some results on the customer journey.

How did we started the project?

Research goal.....

Identify key friction point in the customer journey.

Find out why people cancel so quickly, and bring some solutions to improve the cancellation rate.



Challenges
Time pressure -  I had a very limited amount of time to find out core issues, and propose improvements

Research Approach

- Customer journey mapping

- Hook analysis

- Competitor analysis

- Usability testing

- Unmoderated usability testing

- A/B test


Step 1: Identify key friction points

I combined different methods to identify key friction points in the customer journey which might have an effect on first-time usage.


Step 2 -Usability test to check hypothesis and validate the new direction

After the usability tests, our assumption about the biggest friction-point has been validated. Thus I proposed a new directions on the UI to solve the issue.

Step 3 -  Prototyping 


Build a prototype for an unmoderated study to test different versions of the solutions. 

Find clickable prototype here

How the project ended?

The impact of the research

Though unfortunately, due to time limitations, we could measure yet the results on live production, the project brought closer the different disciplines: CRO, Marketing, and Product. Due to the value of the issues we discovered in the research project, Product management has decided to extend the time for the research and solve the issues and friction points that we have discovered in this project. :)
